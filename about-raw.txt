SFSG,

As SFSG you'll be a supporting role for the SF troops, this can be driving vehicles and logistical support, you can also find yourself providing fire support to the SF in theatre whether its overwatch or Fire Support with GPMGs and other heavy weapons.

JSFAW Ground Crew,

You'll start off as a Ground crew while training to fly rotary airframes such as Pumas, Lynx and Chinooks, after showing competence in flying you can earn the Rank of Sgt and start to fly attack helicopters, after a while you can work towards Fixed wing aircraft if you wish to do so commissioning over to the RAF as a Flight Lt.

Moving up after your intro period
After a few OPs you can then choose to attend training with SAS, SBS or SRR and if successful join their teams or if you wish to be a medic you can train as a medic and join our Medical Detachment.

- Medical Detachment

The 22nd Special Air Service (SAS) Regiment is a highly specialized and secretive unit within the British Army, known for its expertise in counter-terrorism, hostage rescue, and direct action missions. It's the regular component of the UK's Special Forces and is recognized globally for its operational capabilities and training standards.,

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

Counter-terrorism:,
The SAS is often deployed in high-stakes counter-terrorism situations, such as the Iranian Embassy siege in 1980.

Hostage Rescue:,
They are trained in hostage rescue operations, demonstrating their ability to respond to and resolve dangerous situations.

Direct Action:,
The SAS conducts direct action missions, which involve raids, ambushes, and other offensive operations.

- The Special Boat Service (SBS)
The SBS is the Royal Navy's maritime special forces unit, primarily focused on counter-terrorism and amphibious warfare. It is part of the United Kingdom Special Forces (UKSF) and is often described as the sister unit to the Army's Special Air Service (SAS). The SBS is known for its highly classified operations and its ability to conduct covert raids and intelligence gathering.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

-Maritime Counter-Terrorism:
The SBS is the UK's primary unit for dealing with threats at sea, including terrorist incidents and hostage situations.

Amphibious Warfare:,
They are trained in all aspects of amphibious operations, including reconnaissance, raiding, and direct action.

Covert Operations:,
The SBS excels in conducting clandestine operations, often involving infiltration and exfiltration by sea, air, or land.

The Special Reconnaissance Regiment (SRR),
The SRR is a special forces unit within the British Army specializing in covert surveillance and reconnaissance operations. It is part of the United Kingdom Special Forces (UKSF) and operates under the command of the Directorate of Special Forces (DSF). The SRR's role is to gather intelligence in high-risk environments where conventional reconnaissance is impractical or too risky.

The SRR was established in 2005 to provide specialist support for overseas operations, particularly those targeting international terrorism. It was created to meet the growing need for special reconnaissance capabilities.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

Covert Operations:,
The SRR focuses on covert surveillance and reconnaissance, often operating in environments where low-visibility is crucial. They conduct a wide range of classified activities related to covert surveillance and reconnaissance.

Intelligence Gathering:,
A primary function of the SRR is to gather intelligence through surveillance and infiltration teams, identifying targets for other special forces units like the SAS and SBS.

Specialized Skills:,
The SRR employs a range of specialized skills, including close target reconnaissance (gathering intelligence in close proximity to targets) and counterterrorism support.
