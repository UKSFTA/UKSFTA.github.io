The selection process for the Special Forces Communicator is rigorous and covers technical, physical, and tactical domains.

{{% accordionset %}}
  {{% accordion title="Phase 1: Technical Trade Assessment" summary="Evaluation of foundational signal intelligence and communications skills." %}}
  Candidates are tested on their ability to operate standard and non-standard radio equipment under stress.
  - Signal Analysis
  - RF Theory
  - Antenna Construction
  {{% /accordion %}}
  
  {{% accordion title="Phase 2: Physical Aptitude" summary="Eight weeks of intensive physical conditioning and evaluation." %}}
  This phase mirrors the SAS/SBS aptitude but with a focus on long-range patrol communications.
  - Speed Marching
  - Heavy Load Carriage
  - Navigation Exercises
  {{% /accordion %}}

  {{% accordion title="Phase 3: Tactical Communications" summary="Deployment of communications systems in hostile environments." %}}
  Integration with Tier 1 units for live-fire and covert reconnaissance exercises.
  - Covert Comms
  - SATCOM Deployment
  - Tactical Signal Intelligence
  {{% /accordion %}}
{{% /accordionset %}}
